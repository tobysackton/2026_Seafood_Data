How the differential evolved through 2025:
April 2025 $1.50/lb
Peak tariff panic. All buyers competing for limited Gulf supply. Both structural and temporal effects at maximum.
June-July 2025 $0.80-1.10/lb
Gulf season ends. NL at peak production. Temporal effect begins to fade as inventories build.
Sept-Dec 2025 $0.25-0.45/lb
Post-season. Gulf essentially unavailable. Premium reflects secondary market scarcity only.
January 2026 $0.10/lb
Convergence to historical baseline as temporal effects fully dissipate. Only structural premium remains.
Key insight: The extreme differential was not a permanent market repricing but a temporary amplification. Once the tariff-induced rush subsided, prices converged toward the structurally-predicted level of ~$0.50/lb. This pattern—spike then convergence—confirms the two-component model and suggests future tariff threats would produce similar temporary distortions.
